## Manuscript

The strong performance of U90-D50 duration reflects its correspondence to the reproductive phase of rice development. This interval spans from late vegetative growth through complete grain filling, encompassing the critical processes that directly determine final yield. During this period, established tillers transition from vegetative expansion to panicle initiation, proceed through flowering, and accumulate photosynthate in developing grains. The timing of U90 marks when vegetative growth approaches completion and reproductive structures begin formation, while D50 indicates when grain filling nears completion and the plant begins mobilizing remaining resources for final maturation. The interval between these stages thus captures the duration available for the crop to convert accumulated biomass into harvestable grain. Field studies have demonstrated that this reproductive phase length explains yield variation more effectively than instantaneous growth measurements because it integrates the cumulative effects of environmental conditions, resource availability, and plant physiological capacity over the entire yield formation period. The satellite-derived U90-D50 metric effectively captures this physiological duration through continuous monitoring of canopy development, providing a remote sensing proxy for processes occurring at the plant level. This mechanistic correspondence distinguishes duration-based metrics from empirical vegetation indices that correlate with yield without directly measuring yield-determining developmental processes.
