## Manuscript

The strong duration-yield relationship observed in our study aligns with established crop physiological principles regarding the importance of the reproductive stage. Yang et al. (2008) demonstrated that grain-filling duration, rather than filling rate, governs rice yield variation by determining the total radiation and heat units accumulated during this critical period. Similarly, Kong et al. (2026) showed that accumulated Gross Primary Productivity during the heading-to-harvest period accounted for 76% of the variance in rice yield, confirming that cumulative resource acquisition drives grain production. The U90-D50 interval identified in our analysis physiologically corresponds to the reproductive phase, encompassing panicle initiation through mid-senescence. During this window, the crop transitions from vegetative growth to grain development, with photosynthate allocation shifting from structural biomass to grain filling. A longer reproductive duration enables greater accumulation of solar radiation interception and photosynthetic carbon assimilation, directly translating to increased grain mass. The timing boundaries of U90-D50, 90% green-up to 50% senescence, effectively bracket this yield-determining phase, explaining why this specific interval outperforms both earlier and later durations among the 21 metrics tested (Supplementary Table S3).
