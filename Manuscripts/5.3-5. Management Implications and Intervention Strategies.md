## Manuscript

These findings have direct implications for management intervention strategies at multiple scales. At the infrastructure level, water quality management at the Ganwol Lake source, including upstream-downstream mixing systems or development of alternative freshwater sources for downstream pumping stations, could address the root cause of spatial vulnerability. At the field level, zone-specific management approaches differentiated by pumping station coverage areas enable targeted resource allocation. For instance, fields in downstream zones could be prioritized for the selection of salinity-tolerant cultivars or for early-season salinity mitigation measures. Satellite-based phenology monitoring provides operational capability to identify vulnerable areas, generate early warnings through U90 timing detection, and validate the effectiveness of implemented interventions across the landscape. This diagnostic framework thus enables the transition from reactive yield estimation to proactive stress management.
