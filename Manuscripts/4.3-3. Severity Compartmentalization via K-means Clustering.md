## Manuscript

While frequency analysis identifies where vulnerability occurs, clustering provides a complementary approach to establish discrete severity levels across the landscape. We applied K-means clustering (k = 6) to the pixel-wise distribution of U90-D50 duration values across all years, with clusters ranked by centroid values to represent a severity gradient from shortest to longest durations. Shorter duration centroids correspond to greater vulnerability, as they indicate persistently abbreviated reproductive-phase development. The clustering analysis revealed distinct spatial compartmentalization, with the vulnerable zones (Clusters 1-3) concentrated in southern regions,  and non-vulnerable zones (Clusters 4-6) dominating northern portions of the study area (Fig. 7). This spatial pattern closely mirrors the frequency-based vulnerability distribution (Fig. 6), confirming that southern regions experience not only more frequent vulnerability events but also more severe chronic constraints on reproductive-phase duration.
