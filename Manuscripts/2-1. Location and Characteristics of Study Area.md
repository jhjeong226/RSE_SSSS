Data for this study were collected from 'Saedlman,' an agricultural cooperative corporation that cultivates 610 hectares of rice paddies within the Seosan A District. Saedlman operates an integrated production system, collectively managing the entire rice harvesting process. The cooperative's cultivar allocation exhibited significant variability over the study period. From 2016 to 2023 (excluding 2017 due to data unavailability), a total of 45 different cultivars were harvested. Regarding their characteristics, while maturity was predominantly medium and late, other traits such as morphology, resistance, palatability, and purpose were highly diverse. More importantly, the field allocation for these cultivars varied from year to year, making it nearly impossible to directly match field locations and cultivars across years. Given this high year-to-year variability, three cultivars (Golden Queen, Dongjinchal, Samkwang) that were most frequently cultivated were selected for analysis to control for phenological differences arising from cultivar characteristics.

A significant characteristic of the study area is its unique irrigation system. The 610 ha of fields managed by Saedlman share identical climatic conditions and a common geological background as reclaimed land. However, irrigation water is supplied via five major pumping stations that all draw from Ganwol Lake, a freshwater reservoir formed when the reclamation seawall separated this area from Cheonsu Bay. Despite the common water source, the stations' different locations result in varying salinity levels.
