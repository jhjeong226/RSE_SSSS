## Manuscript

Several limitations warrant consideration for future applications. The single-site analysis, while enabling detailed environmental attribution, limits generalizability across different climatic zones, soil types, and management systems. The point-sampled yield data, though operationally realistic, introduced validation uncertainty that spatial heterogeneity amplified for certain cultivars. Future research should validate the diagnostic framework across multiple coastal rice systems experiencing different salinity regimes and irrigation configurations, develop operational yield sampling protocols optimized for satellite metric validation, and integrate the phenology-based approach with process-based crop models to strengthen mechanistic understanding and extend predictive capabilities. Additionally, real-time phenology monitoring systems could enable within-season management interventions, transforming diagnostic insights into operational decision support tools for climate-vulnerable agricultural systems.
