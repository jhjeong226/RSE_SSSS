## Manuscript

These findings establish specific requirements for the successful implementation of phenology-based yield estimation in operational contexts. First, spatial uniformity within target cultivation areas represents a prerequisite rather than merely a desirable condition. Fields exhibiting high phenological heterogeneity require either denser ground sampling to capture spatial variation or subdivision into more homogeneous management zones for separate analysis. Second, satellite spatial resolution must adequately resolve field-scale variability, explaining why 30m Landsat resolution substantially outperformed 500m MODIS resolution for vegetation indices in our narrow-field setting. Third, the metric itself must be validated against cultivar-specific characteristics, as optimal duration windows may shift depending on maturity class or sensitivity to environmental stresses. The success of U90-D50 for GQ and DJ, but not SK, demonstrates that blanket application of duration metrics without considering spatial context risks systematic errors. Future operational systems should therefore incorporate preliminary heterogeneity assessment, possibly through coefficient of variation analysis of phenological metrics across target fields, to identify areas requiring modified sampling strategies or metric selection.
