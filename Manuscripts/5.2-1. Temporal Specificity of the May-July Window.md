## Manuscript

The temporal specificity of salinity effects on U90 timing reveals critical phenological sensitivity windows that align with rice developmental biology. Among monthly environmental variables spanning May through August, only May-July salinity exhibited consistently strong positive correlations with U90 across both the 23-year MODIS record (r = 0.55-0.62, p < 0.01) and the 8-year HLS period (r = 0.75-0.82, p < 0.05) (Fig. 4). Notably, August salinity showed no significant relationship with U90, indicating that this phenological response window closes before the reproductive transition. This temporal pattern corresponds to the early vegetative growth phase in rice, particularly the tillering stage that typically occurs 3-5 weeks after transplanting. During this critical period, the crop establishes its productive tiller population, which ultimately determines panicle number and yield potential. Salinity stress during tillering has been extensively documented to reduce tiller emergence and survival, directly constraining the vegetative foundation for subsequent reproductive development. The termination of salinity sensitivity in August reflects the phenological transition to reproductive primordium initiation, when tiller number becomes fixed and developmental priorities shift from vegetative expansion to panicle formation. This tight coupling between environmental sensitivity and developmental stage underscores why phenology-based approaches enable more precise temporal attribution of stress impacts than season-aggregated metrics.
