## Manuscript

This study developed an integrated phenology-based framework that advances satellite agricultural monitoring from correlation-based yield prediction to diagnostic analysis of environmental constraints. Through systematic comparison of remote sensing metrics, we identified phenological duration (U90-D50) as the optimal yield proxy, increasing explained variance from 6% (vegetation indices) to 27% across diverse cultivars or up to 76% under spatially uniform conditions, by capturing accumulated resource acquisition during critical reproductive stages. Temporal environmental analysis revealed that early-season salinity during May-July dominates control of growth onset timing, while senescence timing reflects complex interactions among multiple late-season factors. Spatial analysis successfully mapped chronic vulnerability zones and attributed observed patterns to infrastructure-mediated irrigation water quality gradients, demonstrating that fixed environmental constraints operating during critical developmental windows determine landscape-scale productivity variations despite annual cultivar rotation.
