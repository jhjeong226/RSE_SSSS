## Manuscript

While U90-D50 duration demonstrated strong yield prediction for GQ and DJ cultivars, the substantially weaker performance for SK (r = 0.525, non-significant) reveals a critical operational constraint. Spatial uniformity within cultivation areas must be considered to ensure metric reliability. This performance discrepancy cannot be attributed to cultivar-specific physiological differences, as all three cultivars share medium-late maturity characteristics and the same reproductive stage importance. Instead, analysis of pixel-level duration distributions revealed that SK exhibited markedly higher spatial variability (mean standard deviation of 6.5 days) than DJ (3.7 days) and GQ (4.4 days) (Supplementary Table S5). Notably, GQ maintained relatively uniform phenology despite occupying substantially larger cultivation areas than SK, indicating that field size itself does not determine heterogeneity. Rather, environmental gradients within cultivation areas, potentially related to differential salinity exposure or microtopographic variation, created spatial phenological variability. This heterogeneity becomes problematic when yield measurements rely on limited point sampling. With only five randomly sampled quadrats per cultivar plot, high within-field variability compromises the representativeness of yield measurements, creating a mismatch between area-averaged satellite signals and point-sampled ground truth.
