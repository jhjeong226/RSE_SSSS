## Manuscript

The consistency of spatial vulnerability patterns despite annual cultivar rotation provides strong evidence for environmental rather than management-based causation. While cultivar allocation and field-specific management practices vary across years, the spatial pattern of vulnerability remains stable and follows the fixed irrigation infrastructure configuration. This indicates that the observed vulnerability is not attributable to cultivar-specific sensitivity or differential management practices, but rather to a persistent environmental constraint mediated by water-source salinity. The infrastructure positioning along the Ganwol Lake salinity gradient thus represents a structural determinant of field-level productivity that operates independently of annual management decisions.
