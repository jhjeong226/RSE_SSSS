## Manuscript

Paradoxically, the ecological complexity that prevents simple D50 attribution creates an operational opportunity for management intervention. Unlike U90 timing, which responds directly and unavoidably to early-season salinity exposure, senescence timing remains amenable to manipulation through late-season management practices. Field-level senescence can be delayed through strategic nitrogen application during reproductive stages, maintained through optimal water management to sustain photosynthetic activity, or accelerated through deliberate drying to facilitate harvest scheduling. This management flexibility exists precisely because D50 responds to multiple interacting factors rather than a single dominant driver, creating numerous potential intervention points throughout the season. The temporal diagnostic findings, therefore, yield a critical strategic insight for coastal rice systems under salinity stress. Management resources should be concentrated on early-season salinity mitigation (May-July) to prevent U90 delay, which represents an irreversible constraint on reproductive duration. Once crops successfully navigate this early window and achieve timely U90, the complexity of late-season controls becomes an asset rather than a limitation, providing flexibility to optimize senescence timing through management rather than remaining at the mercy of environmental conditions. This framework thus transforms diagnostic uncertainty into adaptive capacity, demonstrating how a mechanistic understanding of temporal environmental controls enables strategic resource allocation even in complex systems.
