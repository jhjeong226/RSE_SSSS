## Manuscript

Environmental monitoring data were obtained from governmental organizations of the Republic of Korea. Salinity data for Ganwol Lake were acquired from two sources corresponding to different analysis periods. For long-term phenological analysis (2001-2023), we used salinity data from the Ministry of Environment, which conducts monthly sampling at three monitoring stations (upstream, midstream, downstream) with records since 1997. The mean of three monthly samples represented district-level conditions. For recent high-resolution analysis (2017-2023), we used salinity data from the Korea Rural Community Corporation, which records daily measurements at a single monitoring station. Daily values were aggregated to monthly means for analysis.

Meteorological data from the Automated Synoptic Observing System (ASOS, Korea Meteorological Administration) were used for both analysis periods. Seosan station (Station ID: 129) provided monthly precipitation totals, daily mean air temperature, and daily vapor pressure deficit.

Environmental monitoring data were obtained from governmental organizations of the Republic of Korea. Salinity data for Ganwol Lake came from two sources. The Korea Rural Community Corporation records daily measurements at one station since 2017. Daily values were aggregated to monthly means for recent analysis (2017-2023).

Meteorological data from Seosan ASOS station (Station ID: 129) included monthly precipitation totals, daily mean air temperature, and daily vapor pressure deficit for both analysis periods.
