Environmental data were obtained from multiple sources to characterize growing conditions across the study period. Meteorological data (temperature, precipitation, vapor pressure deficit) were acquired from the Korea Meteorological Administration Automated Synoptic Observing System (KMA ASOS) station located [specify distance] from the study site. Daily observations were aggregated to monthly means or totals for temporal analysis. Soil salinity measurements were obtained from the Ministry of Environment monitoring network, which conducts monthly sampling during the growing season (May to October). Salinity was measured as electrical conductivity (EC, dS/m) in soil saturation extracts from monitoring stations within the Seosan A district. Monthly salinity values represent district-level conditions for temporal correlation analysis with phenological patterns. All environmental variables represent point-based measurements that characterize general district-level conditions rather than field-specific measurements, making them suitable for temporal analysis with district-averaged phenological metrics (Section 3.3).
