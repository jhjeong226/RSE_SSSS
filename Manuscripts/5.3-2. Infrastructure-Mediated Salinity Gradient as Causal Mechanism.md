## Manuscript

The spatial pattern of vulnerability can be mechanistically explained by infrastructure-mediated salinity distribution. Monitoring at three locations along Ganwol Lake revealed a pronounced salinity gradient during the critical May-August period, with downstream stations exhibiting 40.3% higher salinity (2438 ppm) compared to upstream stations (1738 ppm, Welch's t-test p<0.001, Fig. 8). The five pumping stations that supply irrigation water to the study area draw from different positions along this gradient. Southern fields, served by pumping stations positioned closer to the downstream coastward sections, consequently receive higher-salinity irrigation water during the early growing season. This infrastructure-mediated salinity distribution directly corresponds to the observed spatial vulnerability pattern (Fig. 7), where chronically vulnerable zones align closely with downstream pumping station coverage areas.
