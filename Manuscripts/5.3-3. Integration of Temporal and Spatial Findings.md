This spatial finding integrates seamlessly with the temporal analysis of environmental controls (Section 5.2.1). We identified May-July salinity as the dominant temporal driver of U90 timing, and the spatial overlap between delayed-U90 zones (Fig. 7c,7d) and duration-vulnerable zones (Fig. 7a,7b) confirms that this temporal mechanism operates consistently across space. Southern regions repeatedly experience high early-season salinity in downstream irrigation water, leading to systematically delayed growth onset and, consequently, shortened reproductive duration. This spatial-temporal integration validates the diagnostic framework: chronic vulnerability emerges where fields are persistently exposed to the critical environmental constraint during their phenologically sensitive window.
