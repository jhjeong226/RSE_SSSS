## Manuscript

The comparative analysis was conducted at three hierarchical levels using field data from the Saedlman-managed plots, where yield measurements were available. Level 1 analyzed all available field-year observations (n = 68), representing the actual cultivation records across diverse cultivars and years. This dataset includes 45 different cultivars grown over seven years (Supplementary Table 1), with cultivar allocation varying annually according to operational management decisions rather than experimental design. This reflects realistic agricultural conditions where cultivar selection responds to environmental factors, market demands, and management strategies. Level 2 focused on the three most frequently cultivated cultivars (GQ, DJ, SK; n=7 years each, combined n=21) to reduce variability from cultivar diversity while maintaining multi-year replication. Level 3 examined each cultivar separately (n=7 years each) to identify cultivar-specific patterns and assess the influence of spatial heterogeneity on prediction performance.
