## Manuscript

For each field-year observation, satellite metrics were extracted by averaging pixel values within the corresponding field boundary. For Landsat 8 (30m resolution), multiple pixels typically fell within each field, and their values were spatially averaged. For MODIS (500m resolution), pixel footprints often exceeded individual field boundaries, potentially capturing contaminated values including adjacent fields and irrigation infrastructure. Pearson correlation coefficients between satellite-derived values and field-measured yields were calculated for each metric. Statistical significance was assessed at Î±=0.05 for peak vegetation indices and phenological timings. For phenological durations, however, given the large number of comparisons (21 metrics), a Bonferroni correction was applied to control the family-wise error rate (adjusted Î± = 0.05/21 = 0.0024).
