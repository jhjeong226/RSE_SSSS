K-means clustering of U90 timing revealed a spatial distribution broadly consistent with the duration severity analysis, with both the most delayed growth onset and the shortest duration clusters predominantly concentrated in the southern portions of the district (Fig. 6). The 23-year MCD12Q2 and 8-year HLS analyses showed similar spatial patterns, with the most delayed U90 clusters concentrated in the same southern regions across both datasets. Given that early-season salinity was identified as the primary driver of U90 timing in Section 4.2, regions where shortened duration coincided with delayed U90 are associated with the spatial distribution of early-season salinity exposure, whereas regions showing shortened duration without corresponding U90 delay suggest the influence of other contributing factors. Visual comparison with irrigation infrastructure maps revealed that the spatial gradient of vulnerability broadly corresponded to the coverage zones of pumping stations (Fig. 1), which draw irrigation water from Ganwol Lake. Salinity monitoring at three locations along Ganwol Lake showed a consistent spatial gradient during May through August, with downstream stations recording a mean salinity of 2,438 ppm compared to 1,738 ppm at upstream stations (Fig. 7), a difference of 40.3%.
