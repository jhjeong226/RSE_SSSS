Frequency analysis of U90-D50 duration identified southern portions of the district as regions of chronic vulnerability, with the highest recurrence of extreme duration events concentrated in the same southeastern and southwestern areas identified in the severity and attribution analyses (Fig. 8). The 8-year HLS analysis reproduced the spatial pattern identified in the 23-year MCD12Q2 analysis, with high-frequency vulnerability concentrated in the same southern regions despite the substantial difference in spatial resolution and temporal extent. The persistence of this spatial pattern across years with varying cultivar allocation indicates that the identified vulnerability reflects recurring environmental constraints rather than the transient effects of cultivar composition in any given year.
