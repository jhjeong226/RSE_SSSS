A progressive comparison of three satellite-derived metric categories across three hierarchical analysis levels demonstrated that phenological duration metrics substantially outperformed both peak vegetation indices and phenological timings as yield proxies (Table 1). Performance improved consistently as between-cultivar variability was reduced, with U90-D50 emerging as the strongest predictor at each level. Predictive accuracy varied markedly among cultivars, and the degree of within-cultivar spatial heterogeneity was associated with that variation.
