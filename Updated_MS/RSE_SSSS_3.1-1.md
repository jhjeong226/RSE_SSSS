To identify the optimal satellite-based yield proxy, we compared three categories of satellite-derived metrics. Peak vegetation indices and phenological timings represent two conventional approaches widely used in crop monitoring studies. In addition, given the established physiological importance of growth-stage duration for yield formation, we introduced phenological duration metrics calculated as the temporal intervals between phenological timing pairs. By systematically comparing these three categories, we evaluated whether duration-based metrics better capture yield-determining processes than conventional approaches. All metrics were extracted from the datasets described in Section 2.4.
