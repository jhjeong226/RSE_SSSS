Among 21 duration metrics evaluated, U90-D50, defined as the interval from 90% green-up to 50% senescence, emerged as the strongest yield predictor (Supplementary Table S3). This metric yielded strong positive correlations of r = 0.523 (p < 0.001) across all cultivars and r = 0.781 (p < 0.001) among the three repeatedly cultivated cultivars (Table 1, Fig. 2a), both remaining highly significant after Bonferroni correction (α = 0.0024). At the individual cultivar level, the relationship strengthened further, with GQ achieving r = 0.874 (p < 0.05) and DJ achieving r = 0.851 (p < 0.05) (Table 1, Figs. 2b and 2c). U90-D50 spans the period from panicle initiation through grain filling, corresponding to the reproductive phase of rice development. Based on its consistently superior statistical performance across all analysis levels, U90-D50 was selected as the optimal satellite-derived proxy for rice yield estimation.
