These findings carry practical implications for monitoring and stress diagnosis in coastal rice systems. The dominance of early-season salinity over U90 timing identifies a critical phenological window corresponding to early vegetative development, during which environmental conditions most strongly influence subsequent yield-relevant phenological development. Once salinity stress during this window has delayed growth onset and constrained early vegetative development, the resulting developmental deficit cannot be fully recovered through later management interventions, as the vegetative foundation for reproductive development is established during this period. This irreversibility underscores the diagnostic value of monitoring salinity during the early vegetative growth window as a leading indicator of yield risk. In contrast, the complex multi-factor regulation of D50 implies greater management flexibility during the reproductive phase, where irrigation adjustments, nutrient applications, and other interventions can partially modify senescence timing within the constraints established by early-season conditions. The U90-D50 duration metric integrates both the irreversible early-season limitation and the modifiable late-season response into a single satellite-observable indicator, preserving diagnostic utility despite the complexity of individual drivers. Together, these findings demonstrate that phenological metrics can identify not only which environmental factor limits crop development, but also during which developmental stage its effect is most consequential, providing a basis for targeted monitoring and management in stress-prone agricultural systems.

U90-D50 기간 지표는 개별 요인의 복잡성에도 불구하고 진단적 유용성을 유지하면서, 되돌릴 수 없는 초기 계절적 한계와 수정 가능한 후기 계절적 반응을 단일 위성 관측 가능 지표로 통합합니다. 이러한 연구 결과들은 생장 단계 지표가 작물 발달을 제한하는 환경 요인을 식별할 뿐만 아니라 그 영향이 가장 중대한 발달 단계를 파악할 수 있음을 입증하며, 스트레스 취약 농업 시스템에서 표적 모니터링 및 관리의 기반을 제공합니다.
