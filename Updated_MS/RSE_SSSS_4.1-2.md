Peak vegetation indices consistently exhibited limited predictive performance across all tested platforms and metrics (Table 1, Supplementary Table S2). NDVI exhibited a near-zero correlation with yield (r = 0.04, Table 1). Even the strongest performer among all vegetation indices evaluated, Landsat-derived NDWI, yielded a correlation of only r = 0.25. When the same index was derived from MODIS imagery at 500-m resolution, predictive performance deteriorated further to r = −0.09 (Table 1), a result consistent with coarser spatial resolution being insufficient to resolve field-scale heterogeneity in the irrigated landscape. Collectively, peak vegetation indices failed to serve as reliable yield proxies in the study area.
